## Problem Statement

The M9 time-analytics **Day view is THE primary retrospective UI** (the counterpart to the real-time filmstrip/PiP/menu-bar surfaces). Its required interaction model is a **continuous, video-editor-grade flexible timeline**: one time axis the operator fluidly zooms and pans across at *any* granularity — an hour → a day → a week → an arbitrary multi-day span — bounded only by where data exists. There is **no mode** and **no "type a start→end range in a picker to unlock multi-day."** The visible range is *emergent* from zoom level + scroll position; it is not a selection you commit.

The as-built WP6b-4 got the *rendering* right (day lanes, separators, per-day labels, NOW marker, cursor-anchored zoom, Minimap-spans-days — all verified live) but gated the *pannable window* to the picker selection: `deriveDataWindow` returns `[0, day_count*1440]` where `day_count` is exactly the picked span. So in the default single-day view (`day_count===1`) the window is `[0,1440]` and `clampViewport` (correctly, per that window) refuses to zoom/pan out of the one day — to see two days you must go back to the picker and re-type a wider range. That "re-type to unlock multi-day" ceremony is the wrong model. The video-editor model instead makes the pannable/zoomable window a **generous data-bounded span**, and zoom/pan is a **continuous camera** over it; the "range" is what the camera currently frames, and the picker (kept) becomes a **reactive readout + jump-to teleport**, not the gate.

**Problem statement unchanged (F12 back-loop re-check, 2026-07-15)** — the interaction-model goal is confirmed correct: the D1 gate (continuous zoom/pan on the real trackpad) PASSED at verify-human. The one failed leaf (Minimap density misaligned) is a *pre-existing WP6b-1 single-day assumption* the multi-day rework exposed — the Minimap flattens segments by raw minute-of-day (valid only when every `dayOffset` was 0), so it never gained the fixed-origin `dayOffset` shift the DayTimeline uses. This is a localized rendering-consistency fix (P2.7) *within* the same problem, not a shift in it.

## User Stories

- **[PRIMARY — the hot path]** As the operator, I open the Day view and it shows **today** at a legible zoom, and I can immediately **drag-pan backward through days and zoom out to a week/month** — fluidly, continuously, buttery-smooth like a video editor's timeline — **without switching a mode or typing a range**. Panning and zooming is the constant action; nothing gates it.
- As the operator, I want the timeline to **pre-load recent history** (the last ~14 days) so panning back is instant, and to **auto-extend** (fetch older/newer chunks) as I pan toward an edge — so I never hit a dead wall mid-gesture, and I can walk back through weeks of activity seamlessly.
- As the operator, I want the **date-range picker to be a reactive readout of what the camera currently frames** (it updates as I pan/zoom) AND a **precise jump-to** (type a start→end → the camera jumps there, fetching older data if needed) — a convenience teleport, not a gate.
- As the operator, I want each day in a multi-day view to render in **its own horizontal lane** (no overlap), with **day-separator gridlines + per-day date labels**, so a multi-day span is legible — and a **live NOW marker** on today's lane so I can orient "where am I now."
- As the operator, I want the **Minimap** to be the overview map of the whole loaded span (where's the activity, jump there) — across many days it's the primary navigator.
- As the operator, I want **project rows collapsed by default**, and expanding one to **auto-collapse the others** — so I focus on one project's detail at a time and the timeline stays compact (at most one project expanded).

## Acceptance Criteria

The feature is done when:

1. **No mode, no gate — one continuous timeline.** The Day view opens on **today** at a legible zoom and the operator can **zoom out and pan back to reveal adjacent days / a week / up to a month with pure gestures** (drag-pan, wheel-zoom) — with **no picker interaction and no mode switch**. Zooming out past the current day into neighbors is the explicit thing D1 forbade and this AC now requires.
2. **Pre-load + auto-extend the data window (D7).** On open, the timeline **fetches the last 14 days** (not just today) so the recent past is immediately pannable. When a pan/zoom brings the viewport near the **older (left) edge** of the loaded window, the timeline **fetches the previous ~7 days** and grows the window seamlessly (no jank, no viewport jump). Symmetrically, if the loaded window does **not reach today** (e.g. after a jump-to a past range) and the operator pans toward the **newer (right) edge**, it **extends forward toward today**. Extension is seamless: the in-progress gesture is not interrupted and the viewport's framed content does not shift under the operator.
3. **30-day zoom-out cap (D9).** The camera can zoom out until **~30 day-lanes** are on screen; it does not zoom out further (older data beyond that is reached by **panning**, not by zooming past 30 days). "Fit" shows the loaded span up to that cap. This bounds the simultaneously-mounted lane count for gesture smoothness.
4. **Legible open + seed (D6′, supersedes D6).** The view opens framed on **today** at a legible ≈one-day / active-hours zoom (NOT the full 14-day window zoomed-out to slivers). The first gesture is a productive pan/zoom, never a rescue. (D6's "seed to the most-recent day of a picked range" is replaced: there is no picked range at open; the seed is *today*.)
5. **Multi-day coordinate + render (REUSED from P1/P2, must still hold).** Each session's segments render at `dayOffset*1440 + minute-of-day` (`dayOffset = dayOffsetMin(session.day_iso, windowStart)`); each day is its own 1440-min lane, **no cross-day overlap**; the ruler shows **day-separator gridlines + per-day "MMM DD" labels** at day boundaries (intra-day ticks `HH:MM`/`HH:00`; day-level zoom-out labels each tick "MMM DD"). Verified live over a real multi-day span.
6. **Reactive + jump-to RangePicker (D8, supersedes D1/D5's "picker is the gate").** The picker **reflects the currently-framed range**: as the operator pans/zooms, its start→end readout updates to the visible span (day-granular). It is **also a jump-to**: typing/picking a valid start→end **jumps the camera** to that span and, **if the span falls outside the loaded window, extends the fetch to cover it** (the unified model — typed-jump, pan, and zoom all drive one continuous window). An invalid entry (end<start, future end, >30 days) shows the inline error (red border + tooltip) and does not jump/fetch. The picker is NOT a mode switch and NOT required for multi-day.
7. **Single-open project accordion (D10, NEW).** Project rows start **all collapsed**. Expanding a project **auto-collapses every other project** → at most **one** project row is expanded at any time. Collapsing the open one leaves all collapsed. (Replaces the shipped "seed all-expanded, independent toggles" behavior.)
8. **Minimap = overview navigator over the loaded span.** The Minimap density track spans the whole loaded (auto-extended) window; its rectangle tracks/drives the camera across days; clicking/dragging jumps/pans. Expected minimal-to-zero Minimap code change (it reads `deriveDataWindow` + multi-day seg coords) — verify live after the window model changes.
9. **Live NOW marker.** A live vertical "current time" line renders on **today's lane only** (at `nowMin + dayOffsetMin(todayIso, windowStart)`), ticking ~60s. Shows whenever today is within the loaded/framed window; absent otherwise.
10. **Gestures stay buttery-smooth at the 30-day cap.** Drag-pan across day boundaries is continuous (one flat axis, no per-day snap), zoom is cursor-anchored (the minute under the pointer stays fixed), and both stay fluid at the max ~30-lane zoom-out on the **real trackpad** (RAF-coalesced writes; verified live at max span, not just asserted). If a live drag stutters at 30 days, that is a real finding (mitigations: viewport-cull off-screen lanes, memoize per-session rows) — surface it, don't ship jank.
11. **No new tab, no relabel.** Tab bar stays **Day · Week · Month · Compare**. Week keeps its WP6b-3 rollup-grid behavior (NOT routed through this timeline — D4 unchanged). Month drill-down still opens the Day view framed on the clicked day.
12. **Empty-window nav-safety preserved.** A loaded window with no rows renders the inline "No activity" body with the picker/toolbar still visible (the WP6b-3 nav-trap pattern — must not regress). Tracking-OFF still shows the enable-tracking empty state.
13. **All verify gates green:** `tsc --noEmit`, `eslint`, `pnpm vite build` (`GlobalDashboard` stays lazy-split), the full vitest suite (existing pins + new pins for the window/extend/accordion math), `cargo`/clippy (only if a backend min-ts / window helper is added — see D7 impl), and **live verify-self via the MCP bridge**: open → today legible → zoom-out + pan-back reveals prior days with no picker → auto-extend fires at the older edge → picker readout tracks the pan → typed jump-to a past span extends+jumps → 30-day cap holds → single-open accordion → Minimap + NOW correct → single-day today unregressed. Real-trackpad feel is the P-verify-human gate.

- **D8 — The RangePicker is a REACTIVE readout + a jump-to teleport (kept, role changed).** *(operator: "keep but also it should be reactive to pan to reflect the current range" + "jump camera + extend window if needed" on type.)* (a) **Reactive:** the two date fields display the **currently-framed** visible range (day-granular start = floor(visible_start to a day), end = ceil(visible_end)), updating as the operator pans/zooms. Unified: typed-jump, pan, zoom all move one continuous camera over one growing window. Invalid → inline error, no jump/fetch. The `max` cap is **30 days** (D9), was 31.
- **D9 — Zoom-out ceiling = ~30 on-screen day-lanes.** *(operator: "cap to 30 days.")* The camera zooms out until ~30 days are framed; beyond that, older data is reached by panning, not zooming. Caps the simultaneously-mounted lane count (gesture-smoothness bound) and the picker's max span. `clampViewport`'s span cap becomes `min(dataWindow width, 30*1440)` rather than the full data-window width.
- **D10 — Single-open project accordion.** *(operator: "project-rows should be collapsed initially; expanding one auto-collapses the others → at most 1 expanded.")* Rows start **all collapsed** (seed `expandedProjects = []`, not all-ids); `toggleProject(id)` becomes single-open: expanding sets `[id]`, expanding the already-open one (or its collapse affordance) sets `[]`. At most one id in `expandedProjects` at any time. Applies to the timeline body's per-project rows.

"Fit" zooms to the whole loaded span up to the 30-day cap.

## Design / Approach (spec-level — plan will detail phases)

**What is REUSED unchanged (the substrate — do not rebuild):**
- **P1 — `viewport.ts` coordinate math:** `dayOffsetMin`, `formatDayLabel`, `nowMarkerAbsMin`, `viewportPct`, `fracToDataMin`, `panViewport`, `zoomViewport`, `pickTickInterval`, `ticksInViewport` (multi-day branch), `viewportFromRange`/`viewportFromHourRange`, `viewportSeedKey`, `clampViewport` (with a *span-cap* tweak for D9). All pure, all pinned.
- **P2 — the multi-day render engine:** `DayTimeline` (dayOffset lanes / separators / offset-shifted `viewportPct` across all layers), `DayWindowContext`/`DayWindowProvider`, `NowMarker`, the ruler day-labels. Renders any multi-day payload correctly.
- **P3 — `RangePicker.tsx`:** reused as the control; its *role* changes (D8) — it gains a reactive readout binding and its jump-to now triggers window extension. Its `validateRange`/buffered-input/arrow ergonomics stay; `MAX_RANGE_DAYS` 31 → 30.
- **`ViewportContext`/`ViewportProvider`** (generic over `[lo,hi]`, re-seeds on `viewportSeedKey`), **`Minimap`/`minimapMath`** (reads `deriveDataWindow` + coords).

**What CHANGES (the rework surface):**

1. **`viewport.ts` — `deriveDataWindow` + `clampViewport` span cap.**
   - `deriveDataWindow(data)` still returns `[0, day_count*1440]` — but now `day_count` is the **loaded (auto-extended) window's** day count, not the picked span. (No signature change; the *input payload* is the wider window. If a helper is cleaner, add a pure `windowSpanCapMin = 30*1440` constant.)
   - `clampViewport` span cap: `Math.min(windowWidth, MAX_ZOOM_OUT_SPAN_MIN)` where `MAX_ZOOM_OUT_SPAN_MIN = 30*1440` (D9). New pin: a >30-day window can't zoom out past 30 days; a ≤30-day window is unaffected (byte-identical). Pan still reaches the whole window (only the *span* is capped, not the pan bounds).

2. **`GlobalDashboard` — the fetch/window/extend model (the biggest change).**
   - **Open:** replace the "today→today" default with a **14-day window** fetch (`{kind:"custom", start=today-13d, end=today}`) and seed the camera on **today** (D6′). The nav state generalizes from `rangeStart/rangeEnd` (the *picked* range) to a **loaded-window** state `{loadedStart, loadedEnd}` (ISO day bounds of what's fetched) — distinct from the **framed** range (derived from the viewport, drives the reactive picker readout, D8).
   - **Auto-extend (D7):** a viewport-edge watcher (subscribe to the viewport; when `visible_start` nears `loadedStart` or `visible_end` nears `loadedEnd < today`) fires an extend-fetch (prepend 7d / append toward today), merges the new payload into the current one (or re-fetches the widened span — plan picks: a single widened `{kind:"custom"}` re-fetch is simplest and the query is cheap; a stitch is more work but avoids re-transferring), and **preserves the framed content** across the window grow. **Prepending shifts the coordinate origin** (day 0 becomes an earlier day) → every `dayOffset` increases by the prepended day count; the viewport must shift by the same `+prependDays*1440` so the operator's framed minute stays put. This origin-shift-preservation is the crux correctness risk — pin the math (`shiftViewportOnPrepend(vp, prependDays)`), verify live.
   - **Jump-to (D8):** `changeRange(start,end)` (from the picker) → if `[start,end]` ⊆ loaded window, just **set the viewport** to that span (no fetch); else **extend** the loaded window to cover it, then frame it.
   - **Reactive picker readout (D8):** derive the framed range from the live viewport (`framedStart = loadedStart + floor(visible_start/1440)` days; `framedEnd = loadedStart + ceil(visible_end/1440 - 1)` days) and feed it to the RangePicker's `startIso/endIso`. The picker's buffered-input pattern already re-syncs to prop changes, so a pan-driven prop update flows through. (Throttle/round to day granularity so it doesn't thrash on every gesture frame.)
   - **Single-open accordion (D10):** seed `expandedProjects = []`; `toggleProject` → single-open (`[id]` or `[]`). One-line-ish.

3. **The edge-watcher wiring.** Reuse the existing viewport subscription (the Minimap + gestures already read the viewport via context). The extend trigger is a debounced effect on `viewport` compared to `loadedStart/loadedEnd`. Guard re-entrancy (don't fire a second extend while one is in flight) and the `today` ceiling (never extend past today).

**Coordinate-origin note (the load-bearing correctness point):** with a growing window whose *start* can move earlier, `dayOffset = dayOffsetMin(day_iso, windowStart)` is stable only for a fixed `windowStart`. On a **prepend**, `windowStart` moves earlier → all offsets grow → the viewport must be shifted by the same delta to keep the framed content fixed. **Alternative (cleaner, plan to evaluate):** anchor the coordinate origin on a **fixed** reference (e.g. always `windowStart = today-30d` = the max-cap floor, fetched lazily but with a stable origin) so prepending never shifts existing offsets — only reveals already-coordinate-stable earlier lanes. This trades a fixed 30-lane coordinate space (origin never moves) for no shift-math. **The plan picks between shift-on-prepend vs. fixed-origin;** the fixed-origin variant is likely simpler and less bug-prone (no live viewport shift), at the cost of always reasoning in a 30-day coordinate even when less is loaded. Resolve at `/feature-plan`.

**Out-of-scope guards preserved:** overlap detection stays same-project + same-day scoped (the `day_iso` guard makes cross-day pairs correctly non-overlapping); the 6-kind model, dark tokens, lazy-split, the empty-period inline pattern all carry unchanged.

## Open Questions — RESOLVED at `/feature-plan` (2026-07-15)

- [x] **PD1 — Coordinate origin: FIXED origin at `today-29d` (the 30-day-cap floor).** *(operator lean + refined with the code in view.)* The coordinate space is **always** `[0, 30*1440]` (30 lanes from a fixed origin `originIso = stepIso(todayIso, -29)`), regardless of how much is currently loaded. `windowStart` (the coordinate origin for `dayOffsetMin`) is `originIso` and **never moves**. Consequence — the payoff: a backward extend re-fetches a wider span but **every already-rendered lane keeps its exact coordinate** (no re-base, no live-viewport shift, no "jump under the operator"). This is strictly simpler + less bug-prone than shift-on-prepend (which the spec sketched as the risky path). Cost: we always reason in a 30-day coordinate even when 14d is loaded — trivial (unused lanes are just empty). `deriveDataWindow` still returns `[0, 30*1440]`, but the PANNABLE sub-range is bounded to the *loaded* span (a new `loadedWindow` bound), so you can't pan into not-yet-loaded emptiness — reaching the loaded edge triggers the extend (PD-extend below). Since the *coordinate* space is fixed, this "loaded pan bound" is what grows on extend, not the origin.
- [x] **PD2 — Extend-fetch strategy: WIDENED RE-FETCH (not stitch).** *(operator lean, confirmed.)* An extend re-fetches a single `{kind:"custom", start=newLoadedStart, end=loadedEnd}` span (≤30 days, cheap query). Because the origin is fixed (PD1), the re-fetched wider payload's lanes land at identical coordinates — no merge/stitch, no coordinate re-base. Rejected the stitch (avoids re-transfer but re-introduces merge bugs for a query that's already cheap). If a live perf issue appears at 30 days, revisit — but ≤30-day queries against the local SQLite are sub-ms in practice.
- [x] **PD3 — The pannable bound vs. the coordinate window (the load-bearing distinction).** THREE windows, kept distinct:
  1. **Coordinate window** = `[0, 30*1440]`, fixed (PD1). Origin `originIso = today-29d`. `dayOffsetMin(day_iso, originIso)` places every lane; `deriveDataWindow` = this.
  2. **Loaded window** = `[loadedStartOff, loadedEndOff]` in coordinate minutes — the sub-range actually fetched (`loadedStartIso`..`loadedEndIso`). Starts as the last 14 days (`loadedStartIso = stepIso(today,-13)`, `loadedEndIso = today`). Grows on extend. **This is the pan clamp bound** (you can pan/zoom only within loaded data).
  3. **Viewport** = the framed visible span, clamped to the *loaded* window, span-capped at 30 days (D9).
  `clampViewport` gains a `panBound` param (the loaded window) distinct from the span-cap ceiling — or the caller passes the loaded window as the `dataWindow` and the coordinate window only feeds `dayOffsetMin` + the Minimap track extent. **Plan decision: pass the LOADED window as `ViewportProvider.dataWindow`** (so pan clamps to loaded data) and add a separate `MAX_ZOOM_OUT_SPAN_MIN = 30*1440` span cap inside `clampViewport`. The Minimap track spans the loaded window (its natural `deriveDataWindow(data)` — where `data` now covers the loaded span).
