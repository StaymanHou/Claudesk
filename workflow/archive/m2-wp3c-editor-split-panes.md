## Problem Statement

The WP2 `EditorPanel` mounts a single CodeMirror 6 view (`@uiw/react-codemirror`'s
`<CodeMirror>`) in each workspace's half-width right half. WP3c splits that into
**multiple panes** so the operator can view/edit two regions at once (Sublime's
split-editor parity). Within a half-width panel the only sane split direction is
**vertical (top/bottom)**; horizontal would halve an already-narrow column. The
hard part is not the layout â€” CM6 composes multiple `EditorView`s cleanly
(`research.md`) â€” it is **focus management**: the WP3a/WP3b chords (Cmd+S save,
Cmd+Shift+P palette, the future panel-switch hotkey) must act on the **focused
pane**, not "the editor" as if there were one. WP1 already proved N mounted CM6
editors stay within the perf envelope, and settled the chord pattern (capture-phase
`document` listener for app-level chords; `Prec.highest` CM6 keymap for
editor-internal chords) â€” WP3c applies both. **Decision deferred to build, Phase 1
task P1.1:** whether the two panes share one document state (same file, two
viewports â€” the natural choice for a half-width split) or are independent views
(two files); the plan assumes **shared-document** as the default and documents the
verdict at P1.1. This is the riskiest layout/focus slice of the old WP3, isolated
into its own WP so a hard focus problem here cannot block the already-shipped
WP3a/WP3b must-haves.
