## Problem Statement

M9 WP4 adapts claude-time's `viz_data.py` (~1348 lines — events → segment-model JSON) into a Rust query layer that reads the `time_store` SQLite `events` rows, runs the WP3 (redesigned) reclassifier in `src-tauri/src/reclassify/`, and emits the segment-model contract the WP6 dashboard consumes. The emitted shape is the WP1-frozen contract **as revised by WP3's locked 6-kind enum** (`{ai-doing, subagent, ai-reasoning, typing, reviewing, away}` replacing the old `{active, reading, thinking, away, subagent}`) — not a straight port of `viz_data.py`'s current shape. Scope (open sub-decision, resolved here): **global all-projects view with a per-project breakdown** (cross-project "where did the week go", matching the filmstrip/PiP cross-project thesis), rendered from any workspace's tab. Exposed as a Tauri command `time_analytics_query { scope, window }` with a snake_case DTO (NO `rename_all`, matching the `WorkspaceStatusUpdate` IPC-casing convention) + a parallel key-shape test. WP4 also closes 2 MAJOR tail-of-stream correctness findings from the WP3 review that the query layer will consume, removes the dormant-window `#![allow(dead_code)]`, and settles the `chars_per_sec` drop-vs-wire question. `test/test_viz_data.py` (1503 lines) is the **structural oracle** — port the assertions that survive WP3's redefinitions, write fresh where the definitions changed. `build_metrics` / `build_comparison_data` are explicitly OUT of scope (deferred to WP6c, after WP3 defs already locked — they consume `metrics`/`comparison` shapes not needed by the day/week segment surfaces).

- [SURFACED-2026-07-08] Phase 1 (P1.1) — The TRAILING-OPEN-AWAIT MAJOR finding's stated behavioral symptom ("falls through to branch 3 → Away") does NOT currently manifest, because the operator LOCKED `SILENCE_CAP_MS == AWAY_THRESHOLD_MS` (both 10min). With equal thresholds, branch 2 (working-credit → Away when silence > cap) and branch 3 (no-credit → Away when silence > threshold) yield the identical verdict for any silence level, and branch 4 catches short gaps regardless. So the drop was a LATENT bug, not an active misclassification. The WP4 fix (bound the open await at window_end) is kept anyway — it is defensively correct and decouples classification correctness from the threshold-equality coincidence (the moment the caps diverge, the dropped span misclassifies; the bounded one does not). The pinning test asserts the STRUCTURAL guarantee (the span is produced) + the everyday short-gap Reviewing verdict, not a spurious fixed-vs-dropped divergence. Logged to backlog against the finding.
